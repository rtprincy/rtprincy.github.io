Projects

  • Unsupervised machine learning for time-series characterisation

      The aim of this project is to utilise machine learning techniques to identify and characterise the variability of hot subdwarfs (evolved compact stars) observed by the Gaia mission. The t-distributed stochastic neighbour embedding (t-SNE) algorithm was applied to visualise high-dimensional feature space (primarily Gaia DR3 light curve statistical parameters) in a low-dimensional representation, yielding distinctive clusters in the t-SNE 2-D embeddings. The clustering algorithms developed here are both robust in discovering diverse types of new variables and efficient in identifying cataclysmic variables without the need for costly and time-intensive spectroscopic follow-up observations.

  • Frequency analysis of sparsely sampled lightcurves

      In the analysis of sparsely sampled time-series data, a major challenge is accurately identifying periodic signals. When data is sampled at intervals that do not capture all of the signal's variations, noise and artifacts, known as aliasing frequencies, can arise, complicating the estimation of the true frequency. This project aims to alleviate these challenges by introducing a hybrid approach that combines the power spectrum from the Lomb-Scargle periodogram with the Lafler-Kinman statistic, a technique known as the psi-statistic. The resulting periodogram demonstrates the effectiveness of this method when applied to unevenly sampled light curves from MeerLICHT observations.
